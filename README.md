## 🧩 Our Technology

### 💬 Sapiens Chat
**Sapiens Chat** is our next-generation AI system, based on a multimodal model (LLM/LMM), trained on large volumes of data and designed to meet multiple demands — from text generation to multimedia content creation. 🎨📊

---

### 🧠 Schizophrenic AI
Our proprietary approach, called **Schizophrenic AI**, redefines the response generation process.

Unlike traditional models that return the first generated output, our system:
1. Generates multiple initial responses 🧪  
2. Consults specialized auxiliary models 🤖  
3. Performs internal evaluation ⚖️  
4. Selects the most appropriate response for the context 🎯  

This architecture enables a higher level of refinement and adaptability.

---

### 🔗 SAPI (Semantic AI with Pretrained Integration)
The core of our technology is **SAPI**, a proprietary architecture that simulates decision-making processes based on multiple "internal intuitions".

It:
- Integrates various pretrained models 🔌  
- Compares responses semantically 🔍  
- Dynamically selects the best alternative ⚙️

## [About Us]

**SAPIENS TECHNOLOGY LTDA** presented an approach that, since its original disclosure, already anticipated directions that would only later become trends in the development of language models.

Our differentiator lies not only in scale, but in how we think about the response generation process. The proposal called **Schizophrenic AI** introduces a relevant technical concept: a base model (**SAPI**) that **does not immediately return its first output**, but instead compares multiple internal responses, including contributions from auxiliary models, before deciding which is the most appropriate.

This detail completely changes the traditional logic.

While Transformer-based architectures typically prioritize the most probable sequence generated directly, we were already starting from a more sophisticated principle: **evaluating complete alternatives before responding**.

This type of reasoning is similar to what later gained traction with techniques such as Mixture of Experts (MoE), widely used in modern models. However, there is a key difference:

- MoE focuses on **which parts of the model are activated** during generation  
- **SAPI** focuses on **which final answer should be chosen**, after comparing different fully-formed possibilities  

In other words, it is not just a matter of processing, but of **semantic decision-making about the final result**.

Another notable aspect is our proposal of a **virtually unlimited context**. Instead of relying on fixed token windows, a classic limitation of LLMs, we were already pointing toward a system capable of handling large volumes of information without needing to rely on containment strategies.

Later, the market began adopting solutions such as:
- context compression  
- external memory  
- prompt summarization techniques based on attention mechanisms  

These strategies emerged as ways to work around existing limitations. We took a different path from the beginning: **reducing dependence on these limitations directly within the architecture**.

In addition, we were also pioneers in creating a truly multimodal chat model, both in input and output. With the release of the **SAPI-4** model, our proposal already included **interpretation of texts, documents, images, audio, and video**, combined with the **generation of these same formats**, something that, at the time of its disclosure, had not yet been fully implemented by other solutions.

We were also pioneers in applying techniques such as **model distillation** and **low-compute training strategies**, enabling high levels of performance with greater efficiency—an area that only later became central in the advancement of large-scale models.

Additionally, we explored, in a pioneering way, the use of **alternatives to backpropagation** for training models, investigating paths aimed at reducing computational costs and improving learning efficiency—an area that is still considered advanced research today.

In summary, our pioneering character is based on clear pillars:

- **Internal deliberation before the final response**  
- **Integration of multiple models with semantic evaluation across complete responses**  
- **A broad context vision, not strictly dependent on rigid token limits**  
- **Full multimodality (input and output)**  
- **Efficiency through techniques such as distillation and optimized training**  
- **Exploration of alternative methods to backpropagation**  

This combination anticipates an important evolution: models that not only generate responses, but **evaluate multiple possibilities and consciously select the best output**, operating with increasing efficiency and across different types of media.
